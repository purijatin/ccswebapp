Leadership is an art, and in today's environment the practice of management needs to be aligned to the challenges and opportunities that the world provides. This session will seek to answer the question of how business leaders can scale up operations while engaging with customers, building trust with partners, developing programs with governments and motivating internal teams.

CCS 2013

Home

As customers spread out across geographies and cultures, the solutions that businesses develop for addressing their needs need to be scalable as well as customized for each particular segment. To address the dichotomy of scale and customization, leaders need to fine tune their intuition and develop a keen sense of understanding, not just of present customer needs but crucially, in a world with a high degree of technological obsolescence, future customer needs and trends. This session will look at understanding how intuition can feed innovation.

This breakout session is the first of the think tanks to be held across the world to brainstorm on how Leadership Education can be changed/ improved to ensure that business is conducted with human and ethical values. Ideas from the these global think tanks will be consolidated for high impact actions to catalyze the process of embedding values in future leaders before they enter the business arena. Participants in this session, will have an opportunity to engage in this process going ahead.

Humanity has been blessed with an intelligence that has enabled it to implement rapid advances in technologies, lifestyles and effect changes to the world. At the same time, the mind has also been responsible for many of the problems that we experience today - from environmental degradation to war and conflict. This session will look at understanding the human mind and how leaders can harness the power to effect positive social change.

As business functions become increasingly complex, the role of specialists and domain experts has become significant. The deployment of cross functional teams for projects requires business leaders to understand the dynamics of what drives people and how they interact with one another. This session will look at transcending traditional business school theories on employee satisfaction and happiness, and build a theory of how leaders can inspire their teams to achieve excellence.
